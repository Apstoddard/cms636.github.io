## CHRISTINA KREPS--Curatorship as Social Practice
According to Christina, the role of curators has been expanding significantly from merely  caretakeer of the object to an educator, interpretor and designer of the exhibitions and experience of the visitors. It's getting harder to clearly define what exactly a curator is, their work is also highly multi-faceted.

This changing role of curator is only part of a bigger shifting in the whole museum world during the past decades--the shifting significance from object-oriented space to visitor-oriented space. "The problem of decontextualization has been especially acute in the traditional object-centered museum in which objects and collections have served as the core around which all museum activities revolve."
